Alright — now you scroll to the MPTS solution, and this is where the field starts behaving like it should.  
The first step is sizing to your peak reactive demand. In your REACTIVE header tile, the small line reads **1150.1 kVAR MAX**. That’s the peak waste we have to cancel, not the average.

Unity’s MPTS units come in two standard blocks: **H240 at 200 kVAR** and **H490 at 400 kVAR**. Since **1150.1 kVAR** is above 800, this is beyond the off-the-shelf “two H490” range — it requires a **custom configuration** so you’re not under-covered on your worst reactive events. A straightforward build is **3 × H490 units**: that gives you **1200 kVAR** of capacity, enough to cover your **1150.1 kVAR** peak with headroom. Installed cost is simple and transparent: **3 units × $200,000 each = $600,000 installed**.

Here’s what that equipment is doing in plain language. Your motors and inductive loads pull current that’s out of phase with voltage — that’s reactive power. MPTS injects the equal-and-opposite reactive component, in real time, so the transformer and utility stop carrying that “extra” current. That raises your power factor from today’s **82.6%** up to the **99.5%** Unity target. In the chart, what that means is the green layer collapses — not because production drops, but because waste drops.
