PART 2 — HOW WE FIX IT

Now let me show you the fix.

Alright — as you scroll into the solution view, the first number I care about is your **peak reactive demand**, because that determines MPTS sizing. In your header, in the Reactive section, the smaller line shows your **kVAR MAX: 417.8 kVAR**. That’s the peak reactive hit your transformer sees, and that’s what we have to cover.

So here’s the sizing. An **H490** is **400 kVAR** capacity, installed at **$200,000**. Your peak is **417.8 kVAR**, so one H490 alone is just short. We round up — we never undersize. The clean fit is:  
- **1 × H490 (400 kVAR)** plus **1 × H240 (200 kVAR)** = **600 kVAR total capacity**  
That gives you coverage for the peak with safe headroom.  
Total installed cost is **$300,000**.

What this does, in plain terms, is it supplies the reactive power locally — at the right phase angle — so your utility feed no longer has to deliver that waste current through your transformer. That’s how we lift your power factor from **87.2%** up to **99.5%**. And when that reactive current stops sloshing back and forth, two things happen immediately in your field: the green layer collapses, and your heat load drops — which directly reduces the cooling and decay penalty you’re paying every hour.

Now let’s go to what you should expect to see — visually and financially — after MPTS is in place.
