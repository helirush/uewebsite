PART 1 — WHAT I'M SEEING
Welcome. I'm Unity.
You're looking at the January baseline energy field for T12 Main at the Cherry Avenue facility. This chart captures January 1 through January 31 at one-minute resolution.

In this view, dark blue is supplied apparent power in kVA, light blue is useful consumed power in kW, and green is reactive demand in kVAR. The orange trace is power factor.

For T12, the average power factor is 83.7 percent. Average apparent load is 1,250 kVA and peak apparent load reaches 1,766 kVA on a 2,500 kVA transformer. Total monthly energy consumption is 778,982 kWh, with modeled electrical waste of 151,101 kWh, about 16.2 percent of delivered energy.

Reactive demand is the key stressor here. Average reactive demand is about 683.0 kVAR per hour and peak reactive demand reaches 1,088.7 kVAR. That reactive burden increases current, thermal load, and operating friction without creating production output.

PART 2 — HOW WE FIX IT
Now we move to correction strategy with MPTS.
Sizing begins with peak reactive demand, not the average. For T12, peak reactive demand is 1,088.7 kVAR, so a practical configuration is three H490 units for 1,200 kVAR of installed correction capacity.

MPTS injects equal-and-opposite reactive support in real time at the transformer. As reactive burden is neutralized, supplied kVA moves closer to consumed kW and the field harmonizes toward Unity's 99.5 percent power-factor target.

Operationally, that means lower line current for the same productive work, reduced thermal burden in transformer and conductors, and more usable capacity for production.
